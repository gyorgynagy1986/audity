Cookie Policy

Our website uses cookies to improve the user experience and collect information about the use of our website. This policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and the choices you have about the use of cookies on our website.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that a website stores on your device. They are used to remember your settings and browsing history. There are two types of cookies: session cookies, which expire when you close your browser, and persistent cookies, which remain on your device until they expire or are deleted.

2. How do we use cookies?

We use cookies to collect information about the use of our website, such as which pages are visited most often and which links are clicked. We use this information to improve the user experience and better understand our audience.

3. Choices

Regarding the use of cookies on our website, you can choose from the following options:

  • 1 / 3

    Accept all cookies our website uses cookies by default to improve the user experience and collect information about the use of our website. If you do not change your cookie settings, we assume that you accept all cookies.

  • 2 / 3

    Reject all cookies - you can set your browser to reject all cookies. However, we would like to point out that this may prevent some functions of our website from working properly.

  • 3 / 3

    Cookie control - You can set your browser to accept or reject cookies on a case-by-case basis. You can also delete cookies that your browser has already stored on your device.

4. Google Analytics

Google Analytics uses a number of cookies to track user behavior on our website. These include:

  • 1 / 4

    _ga - This cookie is used to distinguish unique users and expires after 2 years.

  • 2 / 4

    _gid - This cookie is used to distinguish unique users and expires after 24 hours.

  • 3 / 4

    _gat - This cookie is used to reduce the speed of requests and expires after 1 minute.

  • 4 / 4

    In addition, Google Analytics uses a cookie called AMP_TOKEN, which contains a token that can be used to retrieve the client ID from the AMP Client ID service. This cookie expires after 1 year.
